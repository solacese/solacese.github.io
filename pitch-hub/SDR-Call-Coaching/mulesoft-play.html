Solace SDR Playbook — Competitive & Objections

“We already use MuleSoft”

They have an iPaaS and think integration is solved. Your job isn’t to argue — it’s to open a door they didn’t know existed. Sell the meeting.

⚡

Core principle: MuleSoft is not the enemy. It’s the thing that makes Solace work better. Every customer story in this space is “MuleSoft + Solace” not “MuleSoft replaced by Solace.” Solace is the layer MuleSoft doesn’t have — real-time event streaming and guaranteed delivery at volume.

1
“We already have MuleSoft”
The Agree-and-Expand
▾
They’re telling you they’ve already invested in integration. They expect you to pitch a replacement. Don’t.
Say this

“That’s great — MuleSoft is solid for API integration. A lot of our customers run MuleSoft too. What they found is that when traffic volume scales up, or they need guaranteed delivery under load, there’s a layer that makes MuleSoft even more effective. That’s usually what’s worth a quick conversation.”

Why it works

Validates their investment — no threat. “A lot of our customers run MuleSoft too” normalises the coexistence. “Makes MuleSoft more effective” reframes Solace as additive, not competitive. The curiosity gap is “what layer?” without naming it.

Don’t do this

Don’t position Solace as an iPaaS alternative. Don’t mention specific MuleSoft limitations. Don’t use the word “replace.” If they think you’re trying to rip out their investment, the call is over.

2
They push — “MuleSoft handles our events too”
Reframe Without Arguing
▾
They think their iPaaS handles everything, including event streaming. They might be right for now. Don’t tell them they’re wrong — make them curious about what “at scale” looks like.
Option A — The volume angle
Say this

“Absolutely, MuleSoft can handle events. What our customers found is that at volume — seasonal peaks, order surges, real-time factory data — there’s a point where an event layer in front of the iPaaS makes everything more stable. One customer went from platform instability to 3.5x more traffic at 30% iPaaS load. That’s the kind of thing worth 20 minutes.”

Option B — The “both” angle
Say this

“MuleSoft is great at transformation and orchestration. What Solace adds is the real-time delivery and guaranteed messaging layer underneath. Think of it as MuleSoft processes the data, Solace makes sure it gets there — even during a spike. Most of our joint customers didn’t plan it that way, but once they saw it, they didn’t go back.”

Why it works

Option A uses a real metric (from the customer base) without naming the account. Option B draws a clean complementary line — transformation vs delivery — that’s easy to grasp. Both position Solace as something that enhances what they already have.

Don’t do this

Don’t say “MuleSoft isn’t designed for event streaming.” Don’t bring up Anypoint MQ specifically. Don’t get into a feature comparison. You’re selling a meeting, not winning an architecture debate.

3
Close or release
Book It or Respect the No
▾
They’re either curious about the complementary angle or they’re done. Read the signal.
If warm — “that’s interesting…”
Say this

“Let me get you 20 minutes with someone who can show you what this looks like alongside MuleSoft — specifically for [their industry]. They can walk through how other companies structured it. What does your calendar look like?”

If cold — “we’re happy with MuleSoft”
Say this

“That’s fair — if it’s working, it’s working. If you ever hit a point where volume or real-time becomes a challenge, we’re an easy conversation. Appreciate your time.”

Why it works

The warm close specifically mentions “alongside MuleSoft” — reinforcing that this isn’t a rip-and-replace conversation. The cold exit plants a seed (“volume or real-time”) without being pushy. They’ll remember when the pain arrives.

Quick Rules for the MuleSoft Conversation

✓ Always

Position Solace as complementary. “MuleSoft + Solace” is the frame, always.

✓ Always

Validate their investment first. They chose MuleSoft for good reasons. Acknowledge that.

✓ Always

Use volume and reliability as the wedge. “At scale” and “under load” are your key phrases.

✓ Always

Sell the meeting, not the product. Your SE will handle the architecture conversation.

✗ Never

Position Solace as an iPaaS. It’s not. Different layer, different job.

✗ Never

Mention Anypoint MQ by name. Let the SE handle the technical comparison if needed.

✗ Never

Say “replace.” Even if the customer eventually does. That’s not your conversation.

✗ Never

Criticise MuleSoft. The person you’re talking to might have championed the purchase.
